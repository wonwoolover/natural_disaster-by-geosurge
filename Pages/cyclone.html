Understanding Cyclones

A cyclone is a powerful circular storm system that forms over warm oceans. It brings strong winds, heavy rain, huge sea waves (storm surges), and floods. Cyclones can destroy buildings, flood villages, and leave thousands of people homeless in just a few hours. One of the worst examples in Myanmar’s history is Cyclone Nargis, which hit in May 2008 and caused a national tragedy.

What causes cyclones?

Cyclones are natural disasters caused by when the the sea surface temperature is very warm, usually over 26°C (79°F). The heat makes water to evaporate, forming big clouds and strong winds. When warm, moist air rises, it creates a low-pressure area below. More air rushes in, starts spinning, and forms a cyclone.

Latest Stories

As of May 2025, Myanmar solemnly commemorates the 17th anniversary of Cyclone Nargis, the most devastating natural disaster in the nation's recorded history. On May 2, 2008, Cyclone Nargis struck the Ayeyarwady Delta with winds reaching up to 215 km/h, leading to catastrophic destruction and a tragic loss of life. People's deaths were reported being more than 22,000, with 41,000 missing during Nargis Cyclone. Organizations like the Mingalar Parahita Orphanage in Phayargyi have provided shelter and education to children orphaned by the cyclone. Efforts have been made to improve infrastructure, establish emergency response protocols, and educate communities on disaster risk reduction.

Four Interesting Facts About Cyclones

Cyclones Spin Differently in Each Hemisphere

In the Northern Hemisphere (like Myanmar), cyclones spin counter-clockwise. In the Southern Hemisphere, they spin clockwise!

Cyclone Nargis Was One of the Deadliest in Asia

It killed over 138,000 people in Myanmar in 2008, mainly due to storm surges and poor warning systems.

Cyclones Can Last for Days

Some strong cyclones stay active for over a week, traveling thousands of kilometers across the sea and land.

Cyclones Are Named

Once a cyclone forms, meteorologists give it a name like “Nargis” to help track and report it more clearly.

⚠️ Three Important Precautions tips for earthquake

Prepare an Emergency Kit

Pack clean water, dry food, flashlight, medicines, and important papers in a waterproof bag—keep it ready during cyclone season.

Stay in a Safe Place

Go to a cyclone shelter or a strong building. Stay away from windows and don’t go outside until it’s completely safe.

Listen to Weather Warnings

Always follow updates on TV, radio, or your phone. If you are told to evacuate, do it early and calmly.

Three Smart Ways to Reduce Cyclone Risks in Myanmar

✦ Build Stronger, Safer Homes

Use stronger materials and smarter designs to make buildings more resistant to strong winds and heavy rain.

✦ Community Education

Teach people, especially in coastal villages, what to do before, during, and after a cyclone. Practice drills in schools and villages can save lives.

✦ Protect Natural Barriers

Mangrove forests near the sea help slow down wind and waves. Plant and protect mangroves along Myanmar’s coastlines.
