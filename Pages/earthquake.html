Earthquakes In Myanmar

Earthquakes can strike suddenly and cause serious damage. Myanmar lies on the edge of the Eurasian and Indian tectonic plates.

Return Back

Understanding Earthquakes

Earthquakes are sudden movements or shaking of the Earth’s surface caused by the release of energy from the Earth's crust. This energy release occurs when rocks break or slip along faults, resulting in seismic waves that cause the ground to shake

What causes earthquakes?

In Myanmar, the main reason why earthquake causes is because of the changes of Tectonic Plate Movement. Myanmar lies at the collision zone between the Indian Plate and the Eurasian Plate. As these plates grind against each other, stress builds up and is eventually released as an earthquake.

Four Facts About Earthquake In Myanmar

Myanmar's Long history of earthquakes

The Bagan earthquake in 1975 severely damaged ancient temples and is one of the most devastating in recent history

Earthquakes cannot be predicted

Scientists or any earthquake professionalists can estimate where they are likely to occur but cannot predict the exact time or magnitude.

Small quakes happen all the time

Myanmar experiences many small earthquakes regularly as an aftershock of the latest big earthquake that occured going up to 7.2 magnitude, which often go unnoticed.

Animals often sense earthquakes before humans

Some animals behave unusually just before a quake, for example like birds, they likely react due to their sensitivity to vibrations and changes in the environment.

⚠️ Three Important Precaution Tips for Earthquakes

Drop, Cover, and Hold On

If indoors during a quake, drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck under sturdy furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops.

Stay Away from Windows and Heavy Objects

These can shatter or fall during a quake. Then they can get you severely injured. Stay clear to avoid injury and protect yourself.

Prepare an Emergency Kit

Have a kit ready water, non-perishable food, flashlight, first-aid supplies, important documents, and phone chargers for at least 72 hours.

Three Smart Ways to Reduce Earthquakes from Happening

✦ Avoid Hazardous Human Activities

Limit construction of dams, deep drilling, or large-scale mining near fault zones to avoid triggering seismic activity.

✦ Apply Earthquake-Resistant Building Codes

Ensure buildings are designed to withstand tremors, especially in high-risk zones like Yangon and Mandalay.

✦ Invest in Seismic Monitoring Systems

Strengthen Myanmar’s earthquake early warning and monitoring networks to detect tremors early and issue timely alerts.
