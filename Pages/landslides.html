Landslides In Myanmar

Landslides often happen in hilly areas like Chin, Kachin, and Shan States during heavy rain. Deforestation make these regions more at risk.

Return Back

Understanding Landslides

Landslides are the downward movement of rock, earth, or debris on a slope. This natural hazard is triggered when the stability of a slope changes due to natural or human-induced factors. Myanmar is highly vulnerable to landslides. Regions such as Chin State, Kachin State, Shan State, and Rakhine State, along with the hilly areas of Kayin and Mon States, frequently experience landslides, especially during the rainy season from June to October.

What causes landslides?

Landslides are natural disasters caused by heavy rains, deforestations, unregulated Mining and construction. Intense rainfall saturates soil, cutting down trees weakens the root systems that bind the soil, making slopes unstable, and Extractive industries and road construction on unstable slopes increase landslide risk.

Latest Stories

On August 13, 2023, a devastating landslide occurred at a jade mine near Manna village in Hpakant Township, Kachin State, northern Myanmar. The incident resulted in the deaths of at least 25 individuals, with 14 others reported missing at the time. The landslide was triggered by heavy monsoon rains. This sudden movement swept miners into the water, leading to the tragic loss of lives. Rescue operations were promptly initiated, involving approximately 150 rescuers utilizing five small boats. The ongoing worry we have is that Hpakant region is notorious for frequent and deadly landslides, particularly during the rainy season. The lack of regulation and enforcement in the jade mining industry has been a longstanding issue, contributing to repeated tragedies.

Four Facts About Landslides

Landslides often occur silently

Unlike earthquakes or storms, landslides may not have dramatic warning signs, making them even more dangerous.

Myanmar has experienced fatal landslides

Myanmar has experienced fatal landslides almost every year. In 2019, over 70 people died in a landslide in Mon State, one of the deadliest in Myanmar’s recent history.

Landslides can trigger secondary disasters

They can block rivers and cause flash floods or create temporary dams that later burst into a real problem that locals or metal miners have to deal with.

Having a wide range of different sizes

Landslides can be very small or very large. Forested slopes are significantly less likely to experience landslides compared to deforested or barren hills.

⚠️ Three Important Precaution Tips for Landslides

Avoid Building on Steep Slopes or Below Cliffs

Settlements and structures should not be placed on or beneath unstable hillsides.

Watch for Early Warning Signs

Cracks in walls or the ground, leaning trees, bulging slopes, and unusual water seepage are early signs of landslide risk.

Prepare an Emergency Kit

Especially during rainy season, have a plan to evacuate quickly and keep essentials like water, flashlight, and first-aid supplies ready.

Three Smart Ways to Reduce Landslides from Happening

✦ Promote Reforestation and Slope Stabilization

Plant trees and deep-rooted vegetation to bind soil. Use terracing and natural barriers to strengthen slopes.

✦ Regulate Construction and Mining Activities

Enforce zoning laws that restrict development in landslide-prone areas. Monitor mining and roadwork to prevent destabilization.

✦ Give community education

Educate local communities about how dangerous can landslides be, signs of landslides, and safety precautions if landslides happen.
