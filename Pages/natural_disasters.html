Types of Natural Disasters


Earthquake (Earth Natural Disaster)

Earthquakes occur when tectonic plates suddenly shift, causing intense ground shaking

Learn more

Landslides (Earth Natural Disaster)

Landslides in Myanmar are becoming increasingly common due to deforestation. Steep slopes make mountainous areas particularly intense.

Learn more

Volcanos (Fire Natural Disaster)

Volcanic eruptions release lava, ash, and gases from deep within the Earth, reshaping landscapes and threatening nearby life.

Learn more

Floods (Water natural Disaster)

In Myanmar (Burma), deforestation and rapid urbanization have increased flood risks. When trees are cleared and wetlands drained, rainwater runs off faster.

Learn more

Cyclones (Water Natural Disaster)

Cyclones bring winds, heavy rains, and deadly storm surges. In coastal regions of Myanmar, where communities live in low-lying areas, these storms can lead to massive flooding like the Cyclone Nargis in 2008.

Learn more
