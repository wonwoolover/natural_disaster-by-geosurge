Understanding Floods

Floods are a common natural disaster in Myanmar, especially during the monsoon season. They occur when heavy rains cause rivers, lakes, or the sea to overflow, inundating nearby areas. Myanmar's geography, with its numerous rivers and low-lying regions, makes it particularly susceptible to flooding.

What causes floods?

Floods are natural disasters that is sometimes caused by the intense rains which can overwhelm rivers and drainage systems and storms like Typhoon bring heavy rains and strong wind.

Four Interesting Facts About Floods

Myanmar Faces Floods Almost Every Year

Especially during July and August, floods affect thousands of people regularly.

Floods Can Happen Slowly or Suddenly

Some floods build up over days, while others like flash floods happen in just minutes.

Floodwater Can Be Dangerous

It often carries germs and trash, which can make people sick with waterborne diseases like diarrhea and skin infections.

Floods Can Destroy More Than Just Homes

They wash away animals, damage schools and hospitals, and ruin fields of rice and vegetables, leading to food problems.

⚠️ Three Important Precaution tips for floods

Listen to Weather News

Always check weather updates during the rainy season. If a storm or flood is coming, move to higher ground quickly.

Keep an Emergency Bag Ready

Have a bag with clean water, dry clothes, food, a flashlight, medicines, and important papers in a waterproof bag—ready to grab and go.

Stay Away from Floodwater

Never walk or drive through floodwater. It may look shallow, but it can sweep people or cars away and hide dangers like open drains or snakes.

Three Smart Ways to Reduce Floods in Myanmar

✦ Use Early Warning Systems

Use mobile apps, sirens, or radios to send flood warnings early so people have time to evacuate and stay safe.

✦ Avoid Building in Flood Zones

Don’t build homes or shops in places that flood every year. Use maps to find safer areas.

✦ Plant more trees

Forest areas should be protected and reforested to prevent floods and landslides. Clean and improve the drains in cities and towns, so rainwater flows out quickly and doesn’t get stuck.
